More information

Who it's for

Techsmiths is for people from any background who want to make the move into the tech sector.

For example, you might be…

  • Working in another sector and wanting a career change, perhaps for a better work-life balance or more challenging role
  • Just out of university or college, and keen to be part of the vibrant tech industry
  • On a career break, and looking to return to work in a way that fits around your life

What you get from Techsmiths

  • A 12-week bootcamp, to train you in the basics of software development and dive into programming languages that will help you hit the ground running on your placement. We also provide training in agile ways of working, DevOPs, software design, testing and relevant soft skills.
  • A personal mentor: Changing careers is a big leap. To support you on this journey, we provide a mentor, who will keep in touch with you throughout bootcamp and your placement, giving career advice and taking care of your wellbeing.
  • Help securing an 18-month paid industry placement, where you’ll be part of a development team, writing code and building up valuable software engineering skills and experience.

Once you complete your placement, the skills and experience you gain will put you in high demand in the job market. You may have the opportunity to continue your career at the organisation which provided your placement. Alternatively, you’ll be well set to secure one of the myriad tech sector jobs in London and beyond.

The Techsmiths package: At-a-glance

  • 12-week software engineering bootcamp in a relaxed environment
  • £30,000 annual salary for the duration of your 18-month placement
  • Pension scheme
  • 20 days' holiday
  • Wellbeing activities
  • You pay a £500 deposit upfront – but this is refunded in full once you secure a placement

Why Techsmiths?

First, your paid placement gives you financial security and priceless industry experience.

Second, we have a strong focus on diversity and recruit people based on their potential rather than solely experience.

And third, you’ll be taught by a team of people who work at the cutting edge of the tech sector, day-in, day-out. We’ve built software to stream Glastonbury’s headliners. We’ve helped shape key UK government cloud computing roadmaps. We worked with DeepMind and Moorfields Eye Hospital to use artificial intelligence to diagnose sight-threatening eye diseases.

You’ll learn first-hand what it takes to build software that can change lives and improve the world we live in.
